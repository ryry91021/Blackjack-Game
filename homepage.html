How to Play Blackjack

Objective: Beat the dealer by having a hand value closer to 21 without exceeding it.

Card Values:

  • Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are each worth 10 points.
  • Aces can be worth either 1 or 11 points, depending on which value benefits the hand more.

Gameplay:

  1. Initial Deal:
    • Each player, including the dealer, is dealt two cards.
    • Players' cards are usually dealt face up, while one of the dealer's cards is face up (upcard), and the other is face down (hole card).
  2. Player's Turn:
    • Players decide whether to "hit" (take another card) or "stand" (keep their current hand).
    • Players can continue to hit until they decide to stand or until their hand exceeds 21, resulting in a bust.
  3. Dealer's Turn:
    • Once all players have completed their turn, the dealer reveals their hole card.
    • The dealer must hit until their hand reaches at least 17 points.
    • If the dealer busts, players who haven't busted win.
  4. Winning:
    • The player with a hand value closest to 21 without exceeding it wins.
    • If a player has blackjack (an Ace and a 10-value card) and the dealer doesn't, the player usually receives a higher payout.
